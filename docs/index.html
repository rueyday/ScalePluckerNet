Scale-Aware PlueckerNet

Extending Plücker Line Registration from SE(3) to Sim(3) with RGB Color Features

Ruey Day

Department of Robotics · University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

Code Paper PlueckerNet (Liu et al., CVPR 2021)

Abstract

PlueckerNet (Liu et al., CVPR 2021) learns to register 3D line reconstructions under rigid SE(3) transformations using a graph neural network over Plücker line coordinates. We show analytically and empirically that its internal SE(3) solver structurally fails when an unknown scale factor is present, as arises in monocular or scale-ambiguous camera systems. The moment term of a Plücker line transforms as m′ = s·R·m + t × d′ under Sim(3), and the SE(3) solver misinterprets the residual (s − 1)·R·m as a spurious translation, yielding corrupted estimates.

We address this with a minimal two-correspondence Sim(3) solver that decouples rotation estimation (SVD on direction pairs) from a joint least-squares solve for scale and translation, and train the unmodified PluckerNetKnn architecture on a synthetic dataset and on the Replica RGBD benchmark. We further extend the input descriptor from 6D Plücker coordinates to 9D by appending per-line RGB color, with 30% color dropout for mixed-input robustness. On the Chess 7-Scenes cross-sequence benchmark with simulated monocular scale (s = 1.8), our Replica-trained Sim(3) model achieves 5.75° rotation error and scale-log-error 0.149, compared with 19.02° and 0.588 for the original SE(3)-PlueckerNet. Replica validation yields 99.2% inlier ratio (6D) and 99.4% (9D color).

Contributions

  • Failure analysis. Analytical proof and experimental verification that the SE(3) Plücker solver fails structurally under Sim(3) — correct rotation, badly biased translation.
  • Sim(3) RANSAC solver. Minimal closed-form solver for (s, R, t) from 2 line correspondences: SVD for rotation, then a 6×4 least-squares system for scale and translation jointly.
  • Real-data training. Trained PluckerNetKnn on Replica RGBD geometry (7 scenes, 4200 pairs) — closes the 89° synthetic-to-real gap to 9.4° on Chess.
  • 9D color extension. Augments 6D Plücker coordinates with per-line RGB; 30% color dropout makes a single model handle RGBD, RGB-only, and mixed inputs. Best val: 99.355%.

Method Overview

The PluckerNetKnn architecture is reused without modification — only the RANSAC back-end is extended from SE(3) to Sim(3).

Sim(3) minimal solver — 2 line pairs

Given R from direction SVD (scale-invariant), per-line moment equation:

s · Rm₁ᵢ − [d₂ᵢ×] · t = m₂ᵢ Stack 2 pairs → 6 equations, 4 unknowns: ⎡ Rm₁₁ −skew(d₂₁) ⎤ ⎡ s ⎤ ⎡ m₂₁ ⎤ ⎢ ⎥ ⎢ ⎥ = ⎢ ⎥ → x = lstsq(A, b) ⎣ Rm₁₂ −skew(d₂₂) ⎦ ⎣ t ⎦ ⎣ m₂₂ ⎦ Hypotheses with s ≤ 0 are rejected.

Part 1 — Why SE(3) PlueckerNet Fails on Sim(3)

When scale s ≠ 1, the SE(3) solver's residual becomes e = (s−1)·R·m₁ for every line. This is non-zero for any choice of t — the solver absorbs the scale mismatch into a wrong translation estimate.

Semantic Object-Centric SLAM

The Sim(3) matcher is wrapped as a SLAM front-end: persistent object tracks across frames create Sim(3) frame-to-frame edges. Evaluated on Replica room2 (150 frames, 3 object tracks) against ground-truth trajectories.
